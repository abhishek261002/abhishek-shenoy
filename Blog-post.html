What is Robotic-Assisted Surgery?

Robotic-assisted surgery is often misunderstood as a procedure performed entirely by a machine. In reality, the surgeon remains in complete control. The robotic arm acts as a highly advanced extension of the surgeon's hands, providing data-driven guidance and a level of physical stabilization that ensures bone cuts are executed exactly as planned.

Before the first incision is even made, 3D imaging technology is used to create a digital "map" of your unique anatomy. This allows us to pre-plan the implant's position with surgical precision, accounting for the unique biomechanics of your joint.

Benefits for the Patient

01. Precision

The robotic system provides real-time feedback, ensuring that the alignment of the new knee joint is perfectly balanced. This reduces the risk of uneven wear and tear over time.

02. Recovery Time

Because the technology allows for smaller, more precise incisions, there is often less damage to the surrounding soft tissue. This typically leads to a faster return to daily activities.

"Precision is not just about the surgery itself; it is about how the patient feels six months, and even six years, down the line."
— Dr. Abhishek Shenoy

Is it Right for You?

While robotic-assisted knee replacement offers significant advantages, it is one tool in a comprehensive surgical toolkit. The decision to use this technology depends on several factors, including the severity of joint degeneration, previous surgeries, and the specific goals of the individual.

During your consultation, we evaluate your gait, lifestyle needs, and clinical scans to determine the surgical approach that will offer you the most stable and natural-feeling result.
